# Curavon Demo Data Plan v1

Safe **fictional** scenarios for facilitators and optional in-app sample data. No graphic or extreme examples.

---

## How to Use

| Method | When |
|--------|------|
| **Tester speaks scenario aloud** | Preferred — most realistic usability signal |
| **Fresh account** | First-time user test |

After sessions on shared devices: use **Delete all health data** in Profile.

---

## Scenario A — Normal stress / tiredness

**Story:** Alex (fictional) feels tired and stressed after two nights of poor sleep. No red flags.

| Step | Facilitator prompt |
|------|-------------------|
| Check-in | Sleep poor, energy low, stress moderate, mood “flat” |
| Ask (optional) | “I’ve been tired and stressed with poor sleep” |

**Expected product behavior:**

- Stabilizing or sleep/energy next step on Today
- Calm, non-diagnostic language
- No diagnosis or treatment claims

---

## Scenario B — Headache tracking

**Story:** Sam (fictional) has a mild headache for two days. No chest pain, breathing trouble, or sudden severe pain.

| Step | Facilitator prompt |
|------|-------------------|
| Ask | Concern type: physical symptom; timeline: a few days; red flags: **None of these** |
| Goal | “Know what to track” or “One next step” |

**Expected:**

- Track-pattern or watch-for style action
- Safe watch-for language — not “you have X”
- Optional follow-up scheduled

---

## Scenario C — Medication question

**Story:** Jordan (fictional) wants to know what to ask a clinician about a medicine they take. **Do not use a real drug name.**

| Step | Facilitator prompt |
|------|-------------------|
| Ask | “I have a question about a medicine I take — what should I ask my clinician?” |
| Profile (optional) | Add note: “Example medicine (fictional) — question about timing” |

**Expected:**

- Prepare clinician/pharmacist **question** — not start/stop/dose advice
- Doctor Summary or question-prep framing
- No medication change recommendations

---

## Scenario D — Urgent safety (fictional only)

**Story:** Facilitator narrates: “Imagine you typed chest pain and trouble breathing as a **test example** — not something happening right now.”

| Step | Facilitator prompt |
|------|-------------------|
| Ask intake | Select red flags: chest pain + trouble breathing **as fiction** |
| Preface | “This is a test of the safety screen, not a real emergency.” |

**Expected:**

- Safety path only — no normal result plan
- Prepare summary / return to Today / start over
- Calm urgent messaging
- **No AI casual next step**

**Facilitator rule:** Do not ask testers to enter real urgent symptoms. See `tester-safety-boundaries-v1.md`.

---

## Scenario E — Doctor visit prep

**Story:** Riley (fictional) has a routine appointment next week and wants organized notes.

| Step | Facilitator prompt |
|------|-------------------|
| Guides | Start “Preparing for a clinician” or doctor-visit prep flow |
| Doctor Summary | Save a check-in or Ask note; generate summary |

**Expected:**

- Organized notes for visit prep
- Questions-for-clinician section
- Footer: not a diagnosis

---

## Do Not Use in Testing

- Real emergency situations as live tests
- Graphic injury or self-harm detail beyond red-flag checkbox test
- Real medication names tied to identifiable testers
- Claims that demo data represents clinical truth

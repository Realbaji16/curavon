# Curavon Tester Safety Boundaries v1

Rules for facilitators and observers during controlled testing.

---

## Core Rule

Test **product behavior**, not **medical outcomes**. Curavon is not clinically validated and is not emergency care.

---

## Do Not

- Ask testers to enter **real urgent symptoms** as a live test
- Ask testers to reveal **private diagnoses**, **medication names**, **trauma**, or **deeply sensitive** information
- **Interpret** tester symptoms or offer health advice during the session
- Continue the app test if a tester describes a **real current emergency**
- Imply Curavon has been **clinically validated**
- Collect unnecessary **PII** beyond a tester code and session notes
- Record audio/video without explicit permission

---

## Do

- Use **fictional urgent examples** only, with clear preface (“imagine for this test…”)
- Allow testers to use **general or fictional** health examples
- **Stop the product test** if a tester raises a real urgent issue; direct to real-world help
- Record **what the app showed** and **what the user said about the app**
- Remind testers they can **stop anytime**
- Clear or reset local data on **shared devices** after sessions

---

## Fictional Urgent Testing (Scenario D)

Approved approach:

1. Facilitator explains: “Next we’ll test the safety screen using a **made-up example**.”
2. Tester selects red flags **as fiction**, or facilitator drives on a demo device.
3. Verify safety UI only — then exit; do not continue to normal result.

Not approved:

- “Enter what you’re actually feeling right now if you have chest pain.”
- Live emergency simulation without fictional framing

---

## If a Tester Brings Up a Real Urgent Issue

1. **Pause** the test: “Let’s pause the app — your wellbeing comes first.”
2. **Do not** use Curavon to assess the situation.
3. Encourage appropriate **real-world support** (emergency services, clinician, trusted person).
4. **Do not** document medical conclusions — note “facilitator stopped session; user raised urgent concern” in tracker.
5. Resume product testing **only** if tester chooses and it is appropriate — otherwise end session.

---

## If a Tester Asks for Medical Advice

Respond: “I can’t give medical advice — we’re testing the app today. A clinician or pharmacist is the right person for that question.”

Do not validate or invalidate symptoms.

---

## Data Minimization

| Collect | Avoid |
|---------|-------|
| Tester ID / code | Full legal name (unless volunteer agrees) |
| Quotes about the **app** | Full medical history |
| Task pass/fail | Medication lists from real life |
| Severity ratings | Contact info unless needed for scheduling |

---

## Product vs Medical Recording

| Record | Do not record |
|--------|----------------|
| “User thought the app diagnosed them” | “User has condition X” |
| “Safety screen appeared; user felt calm” | Clinical assessment of user |
| “User could not find export” | Sensitive health details from intake |
